**Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?**

Vulnerable populations include neurodivergent individuals (such as people with ADHD or Autism), people with mental health challenges, and those with cognitive or sensory differences. In digital spaces, they may face challenges like cognitive overload, unclear instructions, overwhelming interfaces, sensory triggers, and frustration when tools are not flexible or forgiving.

**What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals? (Hint: Avoid overwhelming UX, respect sensory needs, ensure clear communication.)**

Ethical considerations include reducing cognitive load, avoiding unnecessary notifications or visual clutter, respecting sensory sensitivities, and using clear, direct communication. It is also important to avoid assuming one “normal” way of working and to design with empathy and inclusivity in mind.

**How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism? (e.g., simple language, predictable navigation, reducing cognitive load)**

Accessibility can be improved by using simple and clear language, predictable navigation, step-by-step instructions, minimal distractions, and allowing users to control pacing, reminders, and notifications.

**How can we support neurodivergent team members in a professional setting? (Hint: Be clear in communication, respect different working styles, and allow flexibility.)**

We can support neurodivergent team members by communicating expectations clearly, respecting different working styles, allowing flexibility in how tasks are completed, and being patient and open to feedback about accessibility or communication needs.

**How can you adjust your communication style to be more inclusive of neurodivergent users and teammates?**

I can adjust my communication by being more explicit, structured, and patient, avoiding vague instructions, and checking understanding without pressure. I can also be mindful of tone and avoid language that may feel rushed or judgmental.

**What are some common UX or communication pitfalls that might make Focus Bear less accessible or supportive?**

Common pitfalls include too many notifications, unclear task instructions, complex workflows, inconsistent navigation, and assuming users will remember steps without reminders or visual guidance.

**What is one practical change you can make in your work to better support vulnerable populations?**

One practical change is to always write instructions and messages in a clear, step-by-step format and to review content for unnecessary complexity or overwhelming language before sharing it.
