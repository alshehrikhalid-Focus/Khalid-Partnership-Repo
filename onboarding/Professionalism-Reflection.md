## **Research & Learn**

### **What are examples of professional vs. unprofessional behaviour in a casual work environment? (Hint: Think about communication, meeting etiquette, and teamwork.)**

Professional behaviour includes clear and respectful communication, being prepared and on time for meetings, following through on commitments, collaborating openly, and respecting others’ time and boundaries.

Unprofessional behaviour includes ignoring messages or deadlines without explanation, using disrespectful or dismissive language, interrupting others, making inappropriate jokes, avoiding responsibility, or creating discomfort in team interactions.

—------

### **What does respectful communication look like in remote teams? (e.g., tone in messages, handling disagreements, responding to feedback)**

Respectful communication in remote teams means using a clear and considerate tone in written messages, assuming positive intent, addressing disagreements calmly and constructively, responding in a timely manner, and acknowledging feedback even when clarification is needed.

—------

### **How can you give and receive feedback professionally? (e.g., focus on actions, not people; assume good intent; ask for clarification instead of reacting defensively)**

Giving feedback professionally involves focusing on specific actions or outcomes, being constructive, and offering suggestions rather than personal criticism.

Receiving feedback professionally involves listening openly, assuming good intent, asking clarifying questions if needed, and reflecting before responding instead of reacting defensively.

—------

### **Review this guide for working with supervisors**

When working with supervisors, professionalism includes preparing updates or questions in advance, communicating clearly, respecting their time, following up appropriately, and taking responsibility for tasks and progress.
